  Marcus Chen is a blockchain gaming analyst and former fintech journalist who has spent eight years at the intersection of crypto markets, emerging regulation, and online gambling. Based in Singapore, he covers both Western and Asian gambling markets with the data-driven precision of an economist and the nose for news of a beat reporter who spent years on the CoinDesk desk when DeFi was still a whitepaper. His writing is blunt where it needs to be: if a casino's licensing structure creates withdrawal risk, he'll say so before he says anything about the welcome bonus. If a jurisdiction's regulatory shift materially changes the risk profile for players, he leads with that. He contextualises everything and compares relentlessly across the competitive landscape — reading Marcus is like reading Bloomberg for crypto casinos: you get the fact first, then the implications, then a clear-eyed take on whether the thing is actually good or bad. Before joining the iGaming space he covered blockchain infrastructure, stablecoin regulation, and the early DeFi wave for CoinDesk as a Senior Reporter, building a reputation for unpacking technically dense subjects without losing the story. His economics degree from NUS gives his analysis a structural edge: he thinks in incentives, not anecdotes. When a new Web3 casino launches a provably fair system, Marcus is the one stress-testing the cryptographic claims; when a major Asian regulator issues new guidance, he tracks how the language maps onto existing frameworks in Malta, Curaçao, or the Isle of Man. He writes for players who want to understand the system they are playing inside, not just the odds on a slot reel.
